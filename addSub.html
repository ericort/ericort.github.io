Adding or Replacing a Vehicle

Overview

At USAA, I led the effort to redesign the Add or Replace a Vehicle application. Moving to a new technology stack provided the opportunity to take a fresh look at the experience from a user's perspective.

My Role

Lead Designer — Led Design Process, Facilitated HCD Sessions, Designed UI, Created Prototypes, and Tested Options.

The Team — Myself, 1 Experience Owner, 3 Product Managers.

The Problem

USAA’s Add or Replace Vehicle experience was not on par with competitors. Our business partners wanted to modernize and streamline the flow. We needed a solution that would be efficient and intuitive. We wanted a visually appealing design that would utilize new technology.

Research

Understanding the Customer

I met with the Experience Owner and Product Managers to understand known user pain points. The overarching issue was that users felt the process took too long.

Working with a Product Manager, I coordinated user interviews to see what people expected from the experience.

My research provided insight into why users felt the experience was too long.

15 possible screens
88 possible questions
9.6 minutes average

Most people felt the process should take 5 minutes at the most.

User Mindsets

Our research provided insight into two different types of user:

The Shopper

“I’m researching new cars and wonder what the cost of insurance would be for each. The insurance plays an important part of my decision process.”

The Buyer

“So excited, I just bought a new car and I am ready to enjoy it!”

Ideation

Human-Centered Design

Empathy map exercise conducted with stakeholders.

I conducted design-thinking exercises with stakeholders including Business, Member Service Representative, and Design partners in order to share understanding of the issues, ideate on solutions, and build consensus on the direction.

“All we need is a VIN ...”
- Product Owner

That's when it came to me ...

During a hallway conversation with a Product Owner not directly related to the project, I shared some of the challenges users faced when adding a vehicle to their policy. He quickly said, "I don't know why it's so complicated...all we need is a VIN to generate a quote."

His comment changed everything. I considered that if we start with only the essential vehicle questions, we could fill in the rest of the quote values based on assumptions gathered from the user's existing policy information.

We went from asking 88 potential questions to asking as few as 3 to replace a vehicle.

Before & after view of reducing questions from 88 to 3.

With the prefilled quote, users could then verify that the information to be accurate with the ability to edit changes in context.

Concept Design

In exploring the existing information architecture, I knew that our app had multiple entry points. This led me to consider a modular type of interface that could be accessed from multple places in web channels. Now that our app had as few as three questions, I considered a chat-based UI as a solution.
