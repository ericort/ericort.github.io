USAA Auto Quote Redesign

Overview

At USAA, I led the effort to redesign the Quote Results section of the Auto Insurance Acquisition application. This section is where people first see the price of their quote options, learn about their insurance coverages, and are able to make changes. It’s the most pivotal piece of the application where people can decide whether or not to buy the product.

My Role

Lead Designer — Led Design Process, Facilitated HCD Sessions, Designed UI, Created Prototypes, Tested Options, and Ensured Production Quality.

The Team — Myself, 2 other Designers, 1 Producer, and 2 Product Managers.

The Process

USAA is a large organization with many stakeholders, requirements, and processes to coordinate in order to build a solution. Leading with a clear human-centered process helped define business vision and kept the project moving forward. I applied a human-center approach comprised of four phases: Discovery, Concept, Foundation, and Build.

Problem

People were having trouble performing critical tasks such as finding the bundling options, viewing the insurance coverage features, and understanding how to buy the policy. As a result, people would ask for help via the chat or phone channel, or simply drop out of the application.

1. Discovery

Getting Started with Research

I started by conducting stakeholder interviews in order to understand the known pain points. Next, I spent time observing Member Service Representatives who handled phone calls related to Auto. This allowed me to witness first hand how USAA members were interacting with our application. I also reviewed chat logs of users that had issues with the Auto app.

Comparing Against the Competition

I researched how we compared to our competitors by reviewing independent study reports from groups such as Forrester Research, Key Lime Interactive, and J.D. Power. I also performed competitive analysis on other insurance carriers such as Progressive and Geico.

Insights

I synthesized my findings and created a list of key insights which included:

  • USAA includes more steps to acquire insurance than our competitors > Users could be fatigued, so keep it simple.
  • Users that interact with "Coverages" are more likely to purchase > Make coverages more enticing?
  • Users struggled to understand differences between the packages > Offer a comparison option.
  • Users missed the “Bundling” option > Give Bundling more prominence in closer proximity to the price.
  • Users missed the CTA to purchase > Design CTA that follows a more conventional pattern.
“I like the price, but how do I buy?”
- USAA Member

2. Concept

Human-Centered Design Ideation

After gathering insights from the Discovery Phase, I conducted design-thinking exercises with stakeholders including Business, IT, Member Service Representative, and Design partners in order to share understanding of the issues, ideate on solutions, and build consensus on the direction.

Reviewing the Existing Design

I audited the original design based on user observations and data provided by Digital team:

  1. Tabs - not intuatively clickable.
  2. Coverage Glance - probably not needed, consider the cognitive load.
  3. Bundling - easily missed, too far from price.
  4. Primary CTA - uncommon pattern for primary CTA, too far from eye-scan view.
  5. Policy Checker - easily missed.
  6. Coverage Item - can we simplify?

Design Iterations

Design #1 - Design & Lo-Fi Testing

I focused on creating a streamlined UI with a visual hierarchy based on the most important features to users:

  1. Price: Package Options, CTA to Purchase
  2. Coverages: Concise View, Editable, Contextual Help Available
  3. Summary: Brief Details, Editable

I sketched a draft design, critiqued it with my team, and created a higher fidelity flat in Sketch for testing with Invision and Usertesting.com:

Design #1 - Test Results

After testing, the results were encouraging and revealing:

  • Good - Users easily found price & CTA
  • Good - Streamlined UI overall performed well
  • Good - Coverage Glance wasn't missed
  • Bad - UI was too different from the rest of the app ... lost trust
  • Bad - Bundling still too far removed from price

Design #2 - Adjust Design & Hi-Fi Testing

I used the feedback and observations from the user tests and to guide my next iteration of the UI.

3. Foundation

A/B Testing

After learning from our initial tests, we gained favor with our stakeholders to move forward into multi-variate testing with real people on usaa.com.
